FICO® Score and Credit Report 
 
FICO® Score 
Provides you with access to your FICO® Score, developed by Fair Isaac Corporation, which is based on the 
information in your Experian Credit Report at the time it is requested.  Since the information in your 
credit report can change over time, your FICO® Score may also change.  Please keep in mind that credit 
report information can vary from consumer reporting agency to consumer reporting agency because 
some lenders report accounts to only one or two of the agencies (Experian, TransUnion® and Equifax®).  
As a result, your FICO® Score can vary if the information the consumer reporting agency has on ﬁle for 
you is diﬀerent.   
 
The FICO® Score provided through My Credit is based on FICO® Score 8, unless otherwise noted.  There 
are many diﬀerent credit scoring models used by lenders to evaluate credit risk and your lender may use 
a diﬀerent FICO® Score than FICO® Score 8 or another type of credit score altogether.  Just remember 
that your credit rating is often the same even if the number is not.   
 
You must have suﬃcient credit history in your consumer ﬁle with Experian to generate a FICO® Score and 
to receive your Experian Credit Report.  If Experian is unable to match you with a consumer report or is 
otherwise unable to obtain your FICO® Score, you will not be able to use this feature until you have 
suﬃcient credit report history. 
 
FICO® Score Factors and Score Ingredients 
Provides you with details on FICO® Score factors and ingredients to help you better understand how 
certain activities can impact your score.   
 
Score Goal 
Allows you to choose your goal FICO® Score, select a timeframe that works for you, and then receive a 
personalized plan, including unique insights, that provides clear actions you can take to achieve your goal 
based on your Experian® credit data. 
 
FICO® Score Simulator 
Enables you to estimate how your FICO® Score could change if you were to complete certain actions, 
such as paying oﬀ your credit card and/or applying for new credit.  The FICO® Score Simulator allows you 
to enter various data points to see potential ﬁnancial impacts, such as a mortgage calculator , debt 
management, credit card payoﬀ, personal ﬁnance management, and auto loan payoﬀ.  You can 
personalize your credit behaviors and see what would happen to your credit score with the changes.   
 
Experian Credit Report 
With direct access to your Experian Credit Report, which contains information about your credit 
accounts, current balances, payment history, public records, and negative information, you will have the 
information lenders use to make credit decisions and set interest rates.  You will receive an automatic 
update of your Experian Credit Report each month you are enrolled in My Credit and have the option to 
refresh your Experian Credit Report up to three additional times each month.    
 
 
 
 

Credit Monitoring 
 
Experian Credit Monitoring 
Receive alerts when there are key changes in your Experian Credit Report to help identify potential 
fraud.  Key changes include: (1) change of address; (2) new inquiries; (3) new account opening; (4) public 
records; and (5) potentially derogatory information. 
 
Real-Time Inquiry Alerts 
These alerts, delivered in real-time, help you monitor your Experian Credit Report for hard inquiries, 
such as new applications for credit, and notiﬁes you when an event has occurred.   
 
C.L.U.B. Alerts 
These alerts notify you when there are changes to your credit limit, utilization, and balance (C.L.U.B.).  
Experian provides daily monitoring of your consumer report with them and will alert you when: (1) a 
balance on any one of your credit accounts changes by $5,000 or more; (2) the credit usage on any one 
of your accounts changes by 30% or more; and (3) when the credit limit on any one of your accounts 
changes by $100 or more.   
 
Dormant Account Alerts 
Receive alerts when dormant accounts are detected on your Experian Credit Report.  An account is 
dormant if it has a zero balance for six months.  An alert will be sent if activity is detected on a dormant 
account. 
 
Positive Activity Alerts 
These alerts notify you when there are changes to your Experian Credit Report that may positively 
impact your credit score, such as account closure, account payment, account improvement, and account 
status.   
 
FICO® Score Variance Alerts 
The FICO® Score Variance Alert, which can be tailored based on your personal preferences, will notify 
you of changes to your FICO® Score, score rank/risk level, and/or when you have met your personal 
credit score goal.   
• FICO® Score Goal – allows you to set a personalized credit score goal and receive alerts when 
your score reaches your target. 
• FICO® Score Amount Change – allows you to set a personalized point range and receive an alert 
when your score goes up or down by a certain number. 
• FICO® Score Rank/Risk Level Change – allows you to receive an alert when your score changes 
rank/risk level. 
 
IDENTITY MONITORING AND RESTORATION 
The following features are only available if you are also enrolled in the Bank’s rewards program that is 
relaunching on or after May 26, 2026.   
 
SSN Monitoring 
Receive alerts if a new name, nickname or address is linked to your social security number (SSN).  SSN 
Monitoring will provide a report of all names, aliases, and addresses associated with your SSN or your 

child’s SSN.  SSN Monitoring for your children is limited to 10 children under the age of 18 and requires 
veriﬁcation that you are eligible to receive alerts on their behalf. 
 
CyberAgent® Dark Web Monitoring 
This feature scans hidden internet sites, networks, and chat rooms known for illegal commerce of 
consumer personal information.  CyberAgent® searches 15 years’ worth of data, checking thousands of 
websites and millions of data points for compromises to your identity.  After the ﬁrst scan, you will 
receive an alert if a daily database check detects a match to your information.   
 
The feature monitors the following personal information for you and each one of your enrolled children 
under the age of 18 (limited to 10 children): one social security number; one driver’s license number, ﬁve 
national IDs; ﬁve passport numbers; 10 telephone numbers; 10 email addresses; 10 medical ID numbers; 
10 credit/debit card numbers; 10 bank account numbers; 10 international bank account numbers (IBAN); 
and 10 retail credit card numbers.   
 
Account Takeover Monitoring 
This feature monitors ﬁnancial institution data for compromises to your consumer deposit accounts.  You 
will receive alerts if there are new deposit account applications or new open deposit accounts using your 
personal information, changes made to your personal information for an existing deposit account, and 
new authorized users added to your deposit account. 
 
Child Identity Monitoring 
This is a bundled feature, which includes SSN Monitoring and CyberAgent Dark Web Monitoring.  Both 
the SSN Monitoring and CyberAgent Dark Web Monitoring, as described above, are limited to 10 
children under the age of 18 and requires veriﬁcation that you are eligible to receive these alerts on their 
behalf. 
 
Full-Service Identity Restoration 
Identity Restoration helps you and your child(ren) recover from identity theft faster with full-service 
restoration support.  A certiﬁed restoration specialist will investigate alerts and dispute fraud activity to 
help you restore your identity.  In certain cases, the specialist can obtain a consumer-consent limited 
Power of Attorney to further assist you in the restoration process.   For Identity Restoration for your 
child(ren), a one-time parent or legal guardian veriﬁcation will be needed.   
 
Lost Wallet Protection 
Allows you to save and store important payment information, such as your debit card or credit card, and 
identiﬁcation information, such as your passport, driver’s license and insurance cards, in one place – a 
digital wallet.  If the stored cards or documents become lost or stolen, you can cancel or replace your 
cards and update your information within the wallet.  You will also receive support from Experian’s 
Restoration Specialists who can help you cancel and replace or reissue cards and documents.
